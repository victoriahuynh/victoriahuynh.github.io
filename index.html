About Me

Hi! I'm Victoria and I am a current second-year undergraduate student at the University of Washington.

I first became passionate about computer science when I did the Girls Who Code program at Expedia the summer after my sophomore year of high school. I later involved myself in tech in other ways, through AP Computer Science and Cisco Networking Academy at my school and by winning the NCWIT Aspirations in Computing Award for my region. I also spent all four years on my school's FIRST robotics team, working first on the electrical team and the media team, and then as the VP of Marketing. We made it to world championships in 2015 and 2017!

I later had the opportunity to intern at Expedia twice, after my junior and senior years. The first summer, I worked in Expedia Partner Central on the Rooms & Rates Team, and was tasked to update their pages to make them streamlined, comprehensive, and mobile-optimized. The second summer, I worked in Expedia Data Engineering on the Loyalty team and the Partner Marketing team. On the former, I learned about BI development, working with Teradata and Qlikview to run data validation to identify and resolve errors on dashboards. On the latter, I created a dynamic file watcher using Python to review daily data feeds and alert the team when feeds were missing.

Since starting at UW, I've tried to keep myself involved by finding tech clubs on campus to join and attending hackathons. This past summer, I made my own contribution towards closing the gender gap in tech by being a TA for Girls Who Code at Adobe! It was really rewarding going back to the program that started it all for me, this time being the one helping inspire others.

I'm still looking for an internship for Summer 2019, please feel free to contact me!
